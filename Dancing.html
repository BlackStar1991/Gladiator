Gladiator's dancing academy

a centre of excellence for dance

We strive for our students to experience and have a love for several styles of dance. GDA is a perfect fit for students who want a quality dance education while being involved in other activities. Students from ages 6-18 will love our range of classes and most of our graduating students each year have been with us since they were in preschool. Many of our students and familiies have told us that GDA feels more like a family than just a school. This is a wonderful compliment that we take very seriously. Most importantly, we want students at every age and level to love coming to class and to learn far more than just dance steps.

OUR FACILITIES

We have two spacious, fully mirrored, and air-conditioned studios. They are custom built for dancing with no columns or other movement obstructions. Both studios have brand-new Marley flooring. Our downstairs studio has a viewing window inside the waiting room. We also have a Pro-Shop within the studio for your convenience, offering dancewear, shoes, accessories and gifts at discounted prices.

Dancing school for youngs

Our Young Club is invited young people (9 - 16 years old) to learn how to dance. Children and Teenagers can make friends, have fun and develop some great new skills. If you want to learn how to dance, to develop your flexibility and endurance, this club is for you. Our Professional teachers will teach you all the basics of dance

Melissa Balzer

Teaches: Movers & Shakers, Tap, and Jazz

Melissa started dancing at age five. She studied various disciplines such as tap, ballet, pointe, modern, jazz, lyrical, hip hop, and musical theatre. Melissa completed Adapt Jazz and Tap exams as well as RAD and Cecchetti Ballet exams. She attended numerous workshops and dance conventions both locally and across the border including The Edge Performing Arts Centre in Los Angeles and Harbour Dance Centre in Vancouver. Melissa participated in the Manitoba Dance Festival and competed in numerous competitions such as Centre Stage, Canadian National Dance Championships, CanDance, LADM, LADF and the Pulse. She is extremely proud to have been given the opportunity to represent Canada at the World Tap Championships in Germany for two consecutive years. On her second time around she was fortunate to have Lori and Rob as her coaches, which was a wonderful experience for her. Melissa is currently studying at the University of Manitoba in the Faculty of Arts and has intentions of acquiring a Bachelor's degree in Nursing, once her Arts Degree is complete. Melissa is thrilled to be part of the Kickit family and to have the opportunity to share her love of dance with both students and staff at Kickit Dance studio. She is looking forward to another wonderful year at the studio!

Sara Goodman

Teaches: Hip Hop

As an extremely shy 3 year old, Sara began taking dance. It took every ounce of tearful courage she had to go into her class for her lessons, and even more courage to perform in her recital on stage (only while holding her teachers hand). Eventually her love for dance began to outweigh her fears and her dedication grew. Sara's passions in dance are for Contemporary, Jazz & Hip Hop, but her training also includes Ballet, Lyrical, Tap, & Musical Theatre. Sara has been a part of the competitive program at Kickit Dance Studio since 2011. She has competed in Canadian National Dance Championships, Manitoba Provincial Dance Festival, Shobiz Minneapolis, Triple Threat Dance Convention, Candance Regina, The Pulse on Tour in Chicago & Minneapolis, Extreme Dance Convention, & 5678 Showtime. Sara was awarded multiple scholarships & awards during these competitions. In 2014 Sara traveled to Los Angeles California to train at Millennium Dance Complex & Edge Performing Arts Centre. She had the fortunate opportunity to train with Jaja Vankova & Sho-Tyme from SYTYCD. Sara is looking forward to sharing her passion of dance this year!

Erin Ward

Teaches: Tiny Dancers, Tiny/Mighty Dancers, Movers & Shakers, Tap, Jazz, and Ballet

Since starting at Kickit in 2006, and joining the competitive program in 2007, Erin has enjoyed every minute spent inside the studio dancing, as well as outside the studio learning and competing. She has trained in many disciplines including Jazz, Lyrical, Tap, Ballet, Hip Hop, Musical Theater, Acro, and Pointe. Throughout her time at Kickit she competed at many competitions including CNDC, Manitoba Dance Festival, CanDance Regina, and Showbiz Minneapolis. She has also had the privilege of learning from professional choreographers at conventions such as Jump in Minneapolis and Triple Threat. Erin joined the Kickit staff as an assistant in 2014.
Aside from dancing, Erin continues to study Linguistics at the University of Manitoba. She is very excited to share her knowledge and love of dance with the studio!

Jennifer Mariani

Licentiate CSC-CICB; RAD TC; NBS Dip; AISTD


Jennifer Mariani began her training in Harare, Zimbabwe under June Cloete and later joined the School of the National Ballet of Zimbabwe. She continued her training as both a professional dancer and a teacher at Northern Ballet School in Manchester, England. Miss Mariani trained in classical ballet as well as contemporary dance and studied anatomy, music, psychology of teaching and history of dance. She graduated first in her class with Honours. Miss Mariani also holds the NBS teaching Diploma, the RAD teaching Diploma, the ISTD associate Diploma and most recently has attained her Cecchetti Licentiate.


Before moving to Canada, Miss Mariani returned to her native Zimbabwe to perform with the National Ballet of Zimbabwe and the Tumbuka Contemporary Dance Company as well as teaching the Dance Foundation Program whose graduates are drafted into Tumbuka and the National Ballet. She also initiated the Dance for All program which took classical ballet into remote, underprivileged areas of Zimbabwe and orphanages in order to share her passion for ballet with children who do not have the opportunity to dance


Miss Mariani has taught and performed in many countries including Zimbabwe, United Kingdom and Switzerland. In Canada she choreographed the opening ceremony of the British Columbia Winter Games in 2004 as well as numerous other productions for which she received several awards.

Edmund Stripe

Choreographic Associate and Artist-in-Residence, Gladiator's Ballroom School


Edmund Stripe was born in London, England and trained at the Royal Ballet School where his distinguished teachers included Walter Trevor, Nancy Kilgour, Piers Beaumont and Richard Glasstone. A prolific choreographer, Edmund has established a notable career in both contemporary and classical ballet fields as both dancer and ballet master.


Mr. Stripe holds the prestigious Enrico Cecchetti Final Diploma (Distinction) and was appointed an honourary member of the Cecchetti Society of Australia (now Cecchetti Ballet Australia) in 1999.


Mr. Stripe joined Singapore Dance Theatre as Ballet Master in 2000. He assisted in the mounting of works by choreographers such as Jiri Kylian, Nacho Duarto, Stanton Welch, Jan de Schynkel, Boi Sakti, Choo San Goh, Tierry Malandain, Richard Wherlock, Joachim Schloemer as well as continuing to create and remount his own works.


In 2002, Mr. Stripe was invited to join Gladiator's Ballroom School as Ballet Master. Here he assisted with the mounting of works by Jean Grand-Maître, George Balanchine, Christopher Wheeldon, Rudi van Danzig, Kirk Peterson, Agnes de Mille, Emily Molnar and others.


Mr. Stripe’s works for Gladiator's Ballroom School include Unquiet Light (2003), Swelter (2005), the extremely popular Alice in Wonderland (2006) and the critically acclaimed production of The Nutcracker (2008) performed annually by the company across Canada. His work has also been seen in Calgary Opera’s productions of La Traviata and Aida and with the Calgary Philharmonic Orchestra in concert performances of Capriccio Espagnol.


In July 2013, Mr. Stripe was appointed Artistic Director of the School of Gladiator's Ballroom School. In the 2016-2017 Season, Mr. Stripe has taken on a new position with Gladiator's Ballroom School but will continue to work with the School's artistic team for School of Gladiator's Ballroom School productions. This new position will help the School and Company benefit from the collective talents of these artistic teams.

Ashley McNeil

Ashley McNeil, Fellow CSC-CICB, Maestro Cecchetti Diploma


Ashley McNeil’s love of ballet has been a life-long endeavour of commitment and dedication. Ms. McNeil trained at Canada’s National Ballet School, The School of Dance Ottawa and The Quinte Ballet School where she graduated under the direction of Joyce Shietze and Brian Scott.


Ms. McNeil joined the School of Gladiator's Ballroom School as a full-time teacher in 2006. She has also taught for the University of Calgary’s ballet portion of the dance program. In 2011, Ashley was promoted to the Head of the Junior School, Professional Division. In 2013, she was promoted to Ballet Principal.


Ms. McNeil holds her Advanced 2 and Fellowship under the Cecchetti Society of Canada and is the 2011 recipient of the Margaret Saul Scholarship Award. Ashley also holds the prestigious Enrico Cecchetti Final Diploma, (the highest qualification within the method) and received this under the tutelage and artistic guidance of Nancy


Her work to date has rewarded her with many accolades within the dance communities and her students have gone on to dance with Gladiator's Ballroom School, San Francisco Ballet and Ballet Jorgen.
